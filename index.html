I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Center for Neural Science at New York University and a Junior Fellow of the Simons Society of Fellows. My research employs psychophysics, fMRI, and computational modeling to study decision-making and cognitive control.

I received my PhD from Stanford University, where I worked with Anthony Wagner. Before graduate school, I was a research assistant in John Gabrieli's lab at MIT. My undergraduate degree is from Amherst College, where I studied philosophy and neuroscience.

In addition to research, I have been involved with open source software development. I am the creator of seaborn, a Python data visualization library, and I have originated or contributed to several other projects.
